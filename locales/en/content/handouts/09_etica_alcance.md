## Learning outcomes

1. State what STP is for and what it is not.  
2. Separate core claims from Lab extensions.  
3. Map claim strength to empirical maturity.  
4. Cite software, data, domain paper, and hash correctly.

---

## 1. Why STP exists

Systemic Tau Platform is **research and teaching** software to:

- teach the τ_s + RECD grammar against classical EWS;  
- reproduce demos and analyses with hash;  
- transfer the method across domains with explicit maturity.

It is **not**, in v1.0:

- a certified medical device;  
- an operational epidemiological alert system;  
- a trading or social-scoring engine;  
- a substitute for external validation or institutional ethics review.

---

## 2. Technical core scope (v1.0)

### Ready to teach and explore

- Full pipeline τ_s + RECD/excess3 + EWS + surrogates + repro_hash  
- Lab, presets, demo catalog, MD/JSON/Methods export  
- Foundations, mathematics, domains, CCTP demo evidence  
- CLI `stp analyze` / `stp serve`  
- Research library catalog (local publications path)

### Operational Lab extensions vs product horizon

**Already operational in the Lab** (checkboxes / Full mode; not the main claim):

- Breathing window (adaptive W for τ_s)  
- TDA / Betti in windows (optional ripser or VR skeleton)  
- Ordinal memory (symbolic MI)

**Horizon / do not block a thesis on these as the core:**

- Production multi-scale TDA with exhaustive persistence pipelines  
- Symbolic TE / causality as product  
- Billing backends or commercial “plans”

If the core already answers the student’s question, use extensions as **contrast**, not substitute.

## 3. Empirical maturity and honest claims

| Claim | When acceptable |
|-------|-----------------|
| “In this synthetic demo, Δτ_s is extreme vs phase-shuffle” | Always, with hash and design |
| “On the SDDB demo sample, the relational panel moves as …” | With sample and N caveats |
| “The CCTP pilot suggests relational advantage vs ambiguous EWS” | Citing domain work and N limits |
| “Predicts VF / outbreak / market crisis / real polarization” | **No** in v1.0 without external validation and governance |

**Rule:** cardiology anchor strength does **not** automatically export to climate, classroom, or finance.

---

## 4. Third-party data

- PhysioNet / SDDB: comply with ToS and PhysioNet citation.  
- Any real clinical or educational CSV: consent, anonymization, local IRB/ethics.  
- Platform-generated demos: “generated in-platform” license; not field measurements.  
- Research library files: respect original licenses; local paths may contain unpublished material — handle accordingly.

---

## 5. Teaching and assessment ethics

- Grade **process** (design, nulls, dual reading, limits), not only a low p-value.  
- Reward **controls** (logistics vs AR).  
- Penalize commercial or clinical promises without basis.  
- Keep submission hashes for academic integrity.  
- Teach students to write **non-claims** as clearly as claims.

---

## 6. Recommended minimal citation

1. Software: Systemic Tau Platform v1.0 (and aligned paradigm dependencies).  
2. Domain paper/preprint for the analysis domain.  
3. Original dataset.  
4. `repro_hash` if reporting concrete numbers from a run.

---

## 7. Institutional / commercial contact

Commercial or institutional uses beyond local academic use may require a separate license. That does **not** limit learning with local code and course demos.

---

## 8. Decision tree before a public sentence

1. Is the object a demo, a pilot sample, or external data?  
2. Is the claim about reorganization under a declared design — or about prediction?  
3. Are dual panel + null + hash present?  
4. Would a domain expert accept the maturity statement?

If any answer is weak, rewrite the sentence.
